# Summer Web Business — Strategy Learnings

## The Business Model

**Core hook:** Free 24-hour live mockup (GitHub Pages URL) — stronger than a screenshot or Figma because the client can open it on their phone and browse it like a real site.

**Two-tier pricing:**
| Tier | Client type | Tool | Price | Time |
|------|-------------|------|-------|------|
| Generic | Restaurant, salon, trades | Pre-built HTML template | ₱18,000 | <3 hrs delivery |
| Custom | Interesting/unique businesses | Design OS | ₱35,000+ | Open-ended |

**Payment structure:** 50% deposit on mockup approval, 50% on launch. Never 100% at the end — protects against ghosts.

---

## Workflow

**Generic tier:**
1. Identify business type on call
2. Clone pre-built template for that type
3. Swap: name, colors, logo, photos, copy, contact info
4. Push to GitHub Pages → send URL

**Custom tier:** Use Design OS for spec-driven design when the business concept warrants it. Price accordingly.

**Template library (build before first call):**
- Restaurant / café
- Barbershop / salon
- General trades (plumber, electrician, etc.)

---

## Five Blockers Before First Call

Must resolve all five in Week 1:
1. ❌ No finished templates → 24h promise is hollow
2. ❌ No portfolio site → no credibility
3. ❌ No contract PDF → no protection
4. ❌ No pricing number memorized → can't answer "how much?" cleanly
5. ❌ No call script → no consistent pitch

---

## Critical Path (10–12 Week Summer)

- **Week 1:** Template #1 + personal portfolio site live + contract drafted + call script written
- **Week 2:** Templates #2 and #3 done. Calls start end of week.
- **Week 3+:** Delivery + calling in parallel. First client must be signed by Week 3 or the summer math breaks.

---

## The Cold Call

**Wrong pitch:** "I can build you a website."
**Right pitch:** Sell the outcome, not the product.

> *"I noticed [Business Name] doesn't have a website — most people search online before visiting a place like yours. I can have a free preview site built for you in 24 hours so you can see what it'd look like before deciding anything."*

**Three buyer types to address in script:**
1. Sees no value → lead with competitors / missed customers
2. Burned before → lead with "free preview, you decide after you see it"
3. Doesn't know how → lead with simplicity and hand-holding

**Language rules for older clients:** Never say mockup, GitHub, deploy, CNAME, or domain. Plain English only.

---

## Credibility Infrastructure

The #1 conversion barrier is not price or quality — it's trust. A cold call from an unknown student offering fast turnaround + upfront payment reads as a scam to a skeptical older owner.

**Must have before calling:**
- Personal portfolio site with 2–3 example sites visible
- Something to send (email / text) before or right after the call so they can verify you're real
- Professional-looking contact info

---

## Pricing & Upsell Opportunities (activate after workflow is smooth, ~Week 4)

Base site (₱18,000)
→ Google Business Profile setup (+₱1,500)
→ Basic SEO (+₱2,000)
→ Monthly maintenance retainer (₱2,500/mo)
→ Social media graphics (+₱2,000)

One client = potentially ₱30,000+ over summer.

**Referral incentive:** "Refer me to another local business and I'll give you [small perk]." Old business owners talk to each other — one happy client in a business association can cascade fast.

---

## The September Problem

You go back to school in ~10 weeks. Every site you launch creates a support liability. Address this contractually before launch:

> *"I provide 30 days of post-launch support. After that, ongoing maintenance is available at ₱2,500/month, or I can walk you through managing it yourself."*

Options for client handoff:
- Train them to edit the site themselves
- Offer a small monthly retainer for updates
- Transfer GitHub repo to a trusted person they nominate

---

## Hosting & Domain

- **Hosting:** GitHub Pages (free). Custom domain via CNAME.
- **Domain:** Client buys it themselves from Namecheap/Google Domains (~$12–15/yr). You guide them through it.
- **SSL:** Free via GitHub Pages + custom domain (Let's Encrypt auto-handled).
- **Brief clients upfront** that the 24h preview URL will be a temporary GitHub link, not their final domain.

---

## Contract Minimums

Even a 1-page PDF. Must cover:
- Scope (number of pages, what's included)
- Price + deposit structure
- Revision rounds (e.g., 2 rounds of revisions included)
- Timeline
- Ownership clause: site ownership transfers on full payment
- Support window (30 days post-launch)

---

## Council Key Takeaways

- The strategy is sound. The risk is wasting the summer on prep that stretches past Week 2.
- Template library is a compounding asset — delivery gets faster and cheaper every week.
- Don't think about upsells or retainers until the first client is closed and delivered cleanly.
- The unit economics matter: know your call conversion rate and effective hourly rate before scaling.
