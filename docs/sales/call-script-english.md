# Cold Call Script — English

> **Golden rules:** Never say mockup, GitHub, deploy, CNAME, domain.
> Never lead with "I build websites." Lead with the OUTCOME.
> Free preview = "I'll put together a sample site so you can see what it looks like."

---

## Opening (Neutral — works for all buyer types)

> "Good morning/afternoon, is this [business name]?"
>
> "This is [name]. I noticed you don't have a website yet — these days, new customers search online before deciding where to go. I thought I might be able to help."
>
> "I have a free preview I can do — I'll put together a sample site for you in the next 24 hours so you can see it. No charge, no commitment. Just take a look and see if you like it. Can I send you the link on Messenger or text after?"

**If yes →** Get their FB Messenger or number. Hang up. Run the pipeline. Send URL within 24 hours.

---

## Pattern Interruption: Not Sure Who Answered (Owner? Manager? Staff?)

**Critical rule — backed by call data:** Do NOT open with "Are you the owner or manager?" This reads as interrogation and triggers hang-ups (Alch3mist Coffee: hung up immediately on this line before pitch even started).

Instead, let the conversation flow naturally and qualify **after** the opening lands:

> *(After they confirm it's [business name])*
> "Great — are you the one who handles decisions for the business, or should I talk to the owner?"

**If "Yes, I'm the owner/manager" →** Continue with standard pitch below.

**If "I'm just staff / I'll get the owner" →** Do NOT pitch. Pivot to the Staff Gatekeeper branch.

**Why this works over the direct question:** It's embedded in a friendly conversation, not an opener. The prospect has already heard your value proposition (in the opening) before you qualify them — so they understand why you're calling before they decide whether they're the right person to talk to.

---

## Staff / Gatekeeper Branch (Someone Who Can't Decide Answered)

**Signals:** "I'll get the manager," "The owner isn't here," "I'm just working here," "Call back later"

**Rule:** Do NOT pitch to staff — they cannot decide. Give them a deliverable instead of a callback promise.

> "No problem at all — I can send you the sample link instead, so you can show it to the owner directly. Just tap it, it'll open on your phone right away."
>
> *(Get their preferred channel: Viber, Messenger, or SMS)*
>
> "Thanks! I'll send it over now. I'll check back with you tomorrow to see if the owner got a chance to look at it. Thanks again!"

**Key:** Gatekeeper gets a deliverable (link), not a vague "call back later." Follow-up next day anchored to "Did the owner see it?" — not a cold restart.

**Follow-up text if same staff answers again:**

> "Hi again! Were you able to show the link to the owner? Just checking in — let me know if they have any questions."

## Buyer Type 1: "I don't care about a website / not needed"

**Signals:** "We don't need it," "I'm busy," "Let me think about it" (delaying)

**Counter:**

> "I understand, sir/ma'am. But you know, your competitor [competitor name] down the street already has a website. When someone searches '[business type] near me,' they show up first."
>
> "A lot of customers these days don't just walk in anymore — they check their phone first, search where's good. If you're not online, you're invisible to them."
>
> "The preview is free. Nothing to lose. Let's just try it?"

**If still resisting:**

> "Think of it like having a menu that's not in the customer's hands — if they don't know what you offer, they won't come in. The preview is just so they have something to find when they search."

---

## Buyer Type 2: "I got burned on this before" (previous bad experience)

**Signals:** "Someone already did this for me," "Wasted my money," "Paid before, never finished"

**Counter:**

> "That happens a lot, so I completely understand. That's why I do things differently — the preview is completely free. You see exactly what it looks like before you spend a single peso."
>
> "I won't ask for any money until you've seen it and told me you want it. If it works for you, then we talk about next steps."

**Key phrase:** *"No money until you actually like it."*

---

## Buyer Type 3: "I don't know anything about that" (overwhelmed / tech-illiterate)

**Signals:** "I don't know how that works," "You handle it," "What do I need to do?"

**Counter:**

> "No worries at all — I handle everything. The only things I need from you are: the business name, any photos you have, and what contact info you want on the site. I'll do the rest."
>
> "I'll just text you the link when it's done. You tap it, and it opens right on your phone — just like Facebook. No need to install anything."

**Key phrase:** *"I'll handle everything."*

---

## Common Objection Handlers

### "How much?"

> "Right now, the preview is free so you can see if you like it. If you do, the full website costs [price range] depending on what you need. But let's not worry about that yet — take a look at the preview first."

### "Let me think about it."

> "Sure, sir/ma'am. But so you have something to look at, let me just send you a sample link today so you can see what it looks like. Takes about a minute. Where should I send it?"

### "I need to check with my [spouse / partner / child]"

> "No problem, I understand. Can I send you the preview link so you can show them? Or would it be easier if I talked to them directly?"

---

## "Send Anyway" — Default Close for All Non-Hard-No Outcomes

Best-performing tactic from call data. Voice call builds recognition, then link does the actual selling asynchronously. Works through gatekeepers too.

| Outcome | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Owner uncertain | "Let me just send it so you can see it" |
| Staff answered | Send link to staff's device |
| Call dropped mid-pitch | DM link + "I called earlier about..." |
| Wrong number | DM if FB page exists |
| Hard "no" | Do NOT send — move on |

---

## Three-Step Call Sequence (Formalized)

```
Day 1 PM — Cold call (build recognition + brief intro)
Day 2 AM — DM with preview link ("Here's that sample I mentioned")
Day 2 PM — Call with DM context ("Did you get a chance to look at it?")
```

**Key: Voice connection makes DM land harder.** Never DM before first call.

---

## After They Say Yes — Info to Collect

> "Great — here's what I need from you for the preview:"
>
> 1. **Business name** — "What's the exact business name?"
> 2. **Preferred colors** — "Any colors you like? If not, I'll pick something that fits."
> 3. **Delivery method** — "Where should I send the link? Messenger, text, or Viber?"

**Do NOT ask for:** photos, menu, detailed content. Just enough to build the site from what's online + placeholders.

---

## Follow-up Text Template (to send after call)

> "Hi [name]! This is [your name], I called earlier. Thanks! I'll have the sample ready within 24 hours. I'll send the link here once it's done. Anything else you want me to add — a message or different contact info?"

---

## Reminders Before Calling

- ☐ Speak slowly. Grandparent pace.
- ☐ Smile while talking — it changes your tone.
- ☐ Have a glass of water nearby.
- ☐ First 5 calls are warm-up. Don't judge by them.
- ☐ If they say no, thank them politely. No pushback.
- ☐ **Do NOT open with "Are you the owner or manager?"** — let the conversation flow naturally, then qualify after pitch lands.

---

## Converting Preview → Paid Client (Second Call Script)

After sending the preview link, wait 24-48 hours. Then follow up:

> "Hi [name]! Did you get a chance to check the link I sent? What did you think? Anything you'd want to change?"
>
> **[If positive:]**
>
> "Great! When you're ready, here are the next steps: we have a simple one-page contract to sign, and a 50% down payment to start the revisions and publish it live."
>
> "After revisions and contract sign-off, just the remaining 50% before we launch. The website is yours after that."

> **[If hesitant:]**
>
> "No problem at all. What didn't you like about it? We can change anything — first revisions are free."
