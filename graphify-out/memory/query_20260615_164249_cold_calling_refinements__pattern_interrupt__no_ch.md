# Q: Cold calling refinements: pattern interrupt, no-chase close, tonality rules

## Answer

REFINEMENTS FROM OPERATOR (June 16 2026):

1. Pattern Interrupt Opener
   Skip the fluff. Lead with honesty: "Good afternoon, I'm gonna be honest, this is a sales call. You can hang up right now, or give me a moment to explain why I'm worth your time."
   This front-loads friction so the rest of the call isn't shadowed by suspicion.

2. No-Chase Close
   When a prospect goes silent: "Hi [name], this is Daniel. I've reached out a couple of times and received no response. I'll assume you've gone with another provider, which I'm OK with. I wish you the best of luck. If I'm mistaken and you'd still like to call, I'll be glad to help."
   Removes your need for the sale. Reclaims energy. Opposite of chasing.

3. Tonality & Anti-Fluff
   - No "how are you doing today" on cold calls — it generates sales resistance because the prospect knows you don't actually care about their day
   - No buttering up or filler phrases
   - Use tonality shifts to signal confidence, not words

4. Name-First Framing
   Instead of "Is this the owner" (implies stranger = salesperson = cold call = ew):
   "Yes John, this is Daniel with Kanto Web Design."
   Flips from interrogation to introduction. Establishes connection before qualification.

5. Sell the Outcome, Not the Product
   Lead with what changes for them, not what you build. The graph currently reflects product-first scripts but these haven't been updated.
